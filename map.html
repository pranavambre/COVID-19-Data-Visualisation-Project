Globally, the COVID-19 pandemic has now reached more than 2.8 million known cases, spread to 185 countries, and caused more than 200,000 deaths.

More than 7.6 million people have been sickened and more than 420,000 have died of the coronavirus since the start of January, according to NBC News and Johns Hopkins University. COVID-19 cases have been confirmed in at least 200 countries and territories. Close to 60 countries have more than 10,000 cases, and more than 30 countries have reported 1,000-plus coronavirus deaths.

The number of Coronavirus cases worldwide crossed 1.2 million as of April 6, 2020. China, where it originated, now stands fifth in the number of cases.
Four developed nations have recorded a higher number of cases than China– the United States, Italy, Spain, and Germany. The U.S. now accounts for one in five cases worldwide (the highest).
In the U.S., nearly half of the country’s cases tested positive in New York and neighboring New Jersey — an alarming 1,22,139 cases were reported in New York alone.
